History of French

, French was descended from Vulgar Latin of the Roman Empire. When ancient Gaul, now modern France, was conquered by the Romans in the first century B.C., its inhabitants spoke Gaulish, a Celtic language, which was rapidly supplanted by the Latin of the Roman overlords. So although modern French thus inherited many words of Celtic origin and Germanic, it owes its structure and the greater part of its vocabulary to Latin.

La langue française, the language of love, is the first or second language in more than 43 countries and is spoken by 125 million people around the world, on every continent. It is also one of the six main languages of the United Nations.
